      how this was made

      A one-line brief becomes a composed scene in four steps — three of them are an agent talking to itself.

      Nº 01 — the brief

      Sara typed one line.

      No 3D vocabulary. No measurements. No SKUs. Just the corner she wished Mia could sink into.

      Mia wants a quiet reading nook by the window — mid-century vibes, warm tones. An armchair she can sink into, a tall reading lamp, a little footstool, and her current books in a stack on the floor.

      Nº 02 — the decomposition

      An agent rewrote the brief as a parts list.

      Four slots, each with a generation-ready prompt. The agent picked every word below.

      1. armchair a comfortable mid-century modern armchair, warm tan leather upholstery, dark walnut wood frame and tapered legs
      2. floor_lamp a tall slim brass floor lamp with a cream fabric drum shade, vintage style
      3. ottoman a small round leather footstool with short wooden legs
      4. book_stack a small stack of three hardcover books, weathered red and forest-green and cream covers, gold lettering on spines

      Nº 03 — parallel generation

      Lux3D made four objects at once.

      Each prompt went out concurrently. Each came back as a GLB plus a small bag of PBR textures, then was repacked into a self-contained mesh on the way through the pipeline.

      • armchair6m 33s
      • floor_lamp5m 12s
      • ottoman4m 01s
      • book_stack5m 30s

      Nº 04 — spatial layout

      An LLM placed each piece on the floor.

      It read the bounding boxes Lux3D produced and a table of typical real-world heights. It chose a uniform scale per piece so an armchair would read like an armchair next to a lamp that reads like a lamp. Its short rationales:

      • armchair Anchor at origin, turned ~30° to give the scene depth and let the lamp peek over the back.
      • floor_lamp Behind-right of the chair so the shade sits above shoulder height.
      • ottoman In front of the chair as a footrest, ~0.85 m forward of the chair centre.
      • book_stack On the floor to the left of the chair, slightly turned. Three books high.

      Built as 3d-incubators — open source · MIT · agent-driven the whole way down.
